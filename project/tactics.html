Tactics

The first thing to know is this: If both players are making perfect moves, you will always draw.

The solution? Only play against those that have not learned perfect play.

But how does one play perfectly?

The following list of instructions will tell you how.

For each turn, take the first option that is available to you:

  1. If there is an open square that makes three-in-a-row with your shape, take it.
  2. If there is an open square that makes three-in-a-row for your opponent, take it.
  3. If there are two of your shapes on the board in such a way that both the intersecting square and the two other squares in the line are empty, take it.
    In figure 1, we see X has a taken a space in row 1, and the remaining spaces are untaken. They have also taken a space in column 2, and the remaining spaces are empty. This means the best move would be to take the space at row 1, column 2. Please note that column 1, row 3 is also correct in this case.
  4. If there are two of your opponent's shapes on the board in such a way that for each line both the intersecting square and the other square are empty, if you have a way to make a two-in-a-row, make a two-in-a-row, otherwise take the intersection.
    In figure 2, we see that X has taken row 1 column 1 and row 3 column 3, and all the remaining spaces in those rows and columns are vacant. As O has taken center, the correct move would be any of the edge squares as X must then take the opposite edge to prevent O from winning.
    If a corner is taken, X would take the opposing corner thus making a fork and winning the game.
  5. Take the center square if empty.
  6. If the opponent has taken a corner and the opposite corner is empty, take the opposite corner.
    We see this in figure 3: X has taken one corner, so O should take the other.
  7. Take an empty corner square.
  8. Take an empty side square.
